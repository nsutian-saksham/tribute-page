Sir Stephen Hawking

Brilliant Physicist who redefined Cosmology

Sir Stephen Hawking sitting on his wheelchair and a screen in front of him

Time-Line of Stephen Hawking

  • 1942

    (January 8): Stephen Hawking is born in Oxford, England.

  • 1959

    : At 17, Hawking begins his university education at University College, Oxford, studying physics.

  • 1962

    : Graduates with a first-class honors degree in physics from Oxford and begins his graduate studies at Trinity Hall, Cambridge.

  • 1963

    : Diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) at the age of 21, given only two years to live.

  • 1965

    : Completes his Ph.D. thesis on singularities in the framework of general relativity.

  • 1970

    : Develops the "Second Law of Black Hole Dynamics" with Roger Penrose, showing that black holes can only increase in size.

  • 1974

    : Proposes the concept of "Hawking radiation," suggesting that black holes can emit radiation and eventually evaporate.

  • 1979

    : Appointed Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge, a prestigious position once held by Isaac Newton

  • 1985

    : Loses the ability to speak due to a tracheotomy, begins communicating through a computerized voice synthesizer.

  • 1988

    : Publishes A Brief History of Time, which becomes a bestseller and popularizes his work on cosmology and black holes.

  • 2004

    : Hawking retracts his earlier stance on black holes, proposing that information could escape them, revising his own theory.

  • 2009

    : Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian honor in the United States

  • 2018

    (March 14): Stephen Hawking passes away at the age of 76 in Cambridge, England.
